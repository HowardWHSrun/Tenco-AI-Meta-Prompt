5                                                                                       2.1                                            EMI Footprint Test at LIRR                                                                                                                                                  Attend LIRR ROW Safety Training\n\nProvide test staff and test equipment to perform CE, IE and CSI tests at the LIRR sites selected by LIRR, KRC, and Tenco\n\nCoordinate test schedule with KRC.  Ensure M9 railcars to be tested are in good operating condition \n\nCoordinate with LIRR for use of track, train maintenance and operating crews, yard use, shop use, test track time, logistics, security, access, and supervision\n\nProvide M-9 staff including technicians for the EMI Footprint Tests.    Propulsion System supplier provide on-call, remote engineering staff to support EMI Footprint tests, as needed.  Provide Safety Training\n\nSchedule and provide: \n*  LIRR facilities, access, staff including yard track usage and mainline access if needed\n*  Operating and maintenance staff\n*  Signal operations to turn off selected track signals during the test and to connect test equipment to rails if needed; \n*  Safety and flagging\n*  Logistics and coordination.\n\nFor CSI calibration, provide ATC cab signal cal loop and cal box\n\nSchedule, provide direction, and  provide LIRR engineer to witness the EMI Footprint Tests.  Tenco will measure CE, IE and CSI onboard and on the wayside of a LIRR Yard track during daytime hours \n\nProposal assumes 4 test days, 1 contingency day: \n* 2 day for CE\n* 1 day for IE\n* 1 day for CSI.\nSchedule and budget assume 8 useful test hrs per test day, and all test days are consecutive\n\nAssume 4 hours of online safety training for LIRR ROW for 3 Tenco test staff\n\nTest Track wayside area requirements for IE and CSI calibration tests:  Safe space for the test team to setup a test van or a table and chairs with shelter from rain, snow, wind and sun\n\nTest train requirements: Provide onboard PTU during the test, and coordinate with LIRR to operate M-9 train in ATC bypass mode during test. LIRR and KRC will coordinate AW3 physical loading, or simulated propulsion software for AW3 powering and braking tractive effort\n\nTenco will provide a configured TransDAS\n\nIf needed, as added scope, work with KRC and suppliers to resolve EMI issues shown in tests.
